# Aurora Dynamics Employee Handbook

**Version 4.2 — Effective January 1, 2026**
**Owner: People Operations (people@auroradynamics.example)**

Welcome to Aurora Dynamics. This handbook covers the policies you will use most:
time off, remote work, expenses, security, onboarding, and benefits. It applies to
all full-time employees in the United States and Canada. Where local law is more
generous than this handbook, local law wins. Questions go to People Operations;
policy exceptions require written approval from your department head.

---

## 1. Paid Time Off (PTO)

Aurora Dynamics provides **24 days of PTO per year** for all full-time employees,
regardless of tenure or level. PTO accrues at a rate of **2 days per month**,
beginning on your first day of employment. There is no waiting period: you may
use PTO as soon as it accrues.

Carryover rules:

- You may **carry over a maximum of 5 unused PTO days** into the following
  calendar year.
- Carried-over days expire if not used by **March 31** of the new year.
- Days beyond the 5-day carryover cap are forfeited on December 31. Aurora does
  not pay out forfeited days except where required by state or provincial law.

Requesting PTO: submit requests in Workline (our HRIS) at least **10 business
days in advance** for absences of 5 or more consecutive days. Shorter absences
require only your manager's approval, which should not be unreasonably withheld.
Managers may not ask for a reason; PTO is yours to use.

Sick leave is tracked separately from PTO. Every employee receives **10 paid
sick days per year**, which do not carry over and are not paid out. Sick days
may also be used to care for an ill family member.

Parental leave: Aurora provides **16 weeks of fully paid leave for primary
caregivers** and **6 weeks for secondary caregivers**, usable within 12 months
of birth, adoption, or foster placement. Parental leave does not draw down PTO.

Company holidays: Aurora observes **11 paid company holidays** per year, plus a
company-wide shutdown between December 25 and January 1.

### 1.1 Other Leave

- **Sabbatical:** after **5 years of continuous service**, employees are
  eligible for a **6-week paid sabbatical**, usable within the following two
  years. Sabbaticals require 90 days' notice and department-head approval of
  the coverage plan.
- **Bereavement leave:** 10 paid days for immediate family, 3 days for
  extended family, in addition to PTO.
- **Jury duty and voting:** fully paid for the duration of service; no PTO
  drawdown.
- **Military leave:** administered per USERRA in the US and the equivalent
  provincial rules in Canada; Aurora tops up pay to 100% of base salary for
  up to 12 weeks per year.
- **Unpaid personal leave:** up to 12 weeks may be granted at department-head
  discretion after one year of service; benefits continue with the employee
  paying the employee-share of premiums.

## 2. Remote and Hybrid Work

Aurora operates on a **hybrid model**. Employees within 50 km of an Aurora
office are expected in the office a **minimum of 2 days per week**. **Tuesday
and Thursday are the designated anchor days** for all teams, chosen so that
cross-functional collaboration lands on the same days company-wide.

Fully remote arrangements are the exception, not the rule. A **fully remote**
arrangement requires **written VP approval** and an annual review. Approved
remote employees must be reachable during their team's core hours of
**10:00–15:00 in the team's home time zone**.

Remote work support:

- A **$600 per year remote work stipend**, paid quarterly, covering internet
  and incidental costs.
- A **one-time $1,200 home office equipment budget** for new hires, spent
  through the IT equipment portal (monitors, chairs, desks, peripherals).
- Aurora does not reimburse coworking space memberships unless pre-approved by
  a department head.

Working from another country for more than **30 days per calendar year**
requires prior approval from Legal and People Operations due to tax and
employment-law exposure.

## 3. Expenses and Travel

All expenses are submitted through **Ledgerline** within **30 days** of being
incurred. Receipts are required for any expense over **$25**. Expense reports
older than 90 days are not reimbursable without CFO approval.

Approval thresholds:

- Expenses **under $500**: manager approval.
- Expenses of **$500 or more**: department head approval.
- Any single expense over **$5,000**: CFO approval.

Travel limits:

- Meals: **$75 per day for domestic travel**, **$110 per day for international
  travel**. Alcohol is not reimbursable.
- Lodging: hotel rooms are capped at **$250 per night**, excluding taxes.
  Exceptions for high-cost cities (New York, San Francisco, London, Tokyo)
  raise the cap to **$350 per night**.
- Flights: **economy class for flights under 6 hours**; **premium economy is
  permitted for flights over 6 hours**. Business class requires VP approval.
- Ground transport: rideshare and public transit are reimbursable; rental cars
  require a business justification.

Client entertainment is capped at **$150 per attendee per event** and must list
every attendee in the Ledgerline report.

Booking: all flights and hotels must be booked through the **Ledgerline Travel
portal** so that itineraries feed the duty-of-care system. Personal-card
bookings outside the portal are reimbursed only for genuine emergencies.
Travel booked more than 14 days in advance is auto-approved within the limits
above; last-minute bookings route to the department head. Trip insurance,
checked bags (one per traveler), seat selection, and in-flight Wi-Fi for
flights over 3 hours are reimbursable; lounge access and upgrades are not.

## 4. Information Security

Security is a condition of employment. The policies below are audited quarterly
by the Security team, and violations are escalated to People Operations.

Passwords and authentication:

- Passwords must be at least **14 characters** and unique per system.
- **Password rotation is required every 90 days** for all production and
  administrative systems. Personal workstation passwords follow the same
  90-day rotation schedule.
- **Two-factor authentication (2FA) is mandatory on all company systems.**
  Engineering and Operations staff must use **hardware security keys
  (YubiKey 5 series)**; all other employees may use an approved authenticator
  app. SMS-based 2FA is prohibited.
- Shared accounts are prohibited. Service accounts must be owned by a named
  engineer and registered in the asset inventory.

Devices:

- Company laptops must run full-disk encryption: **FileVault on macOS,
  BitLocker on Windows**. Encryption is enforced by MDM and may not be
  disabled.
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported to **security@auroradynamics.example
  within 24 hours**.
- Personal devices may access email and calendar only through the MDM-enrolled
  mobile profile.

Training and incidents:

- All employees complete **quarterly phishing-awareness training**; new hires
  complete **security training within their first week**.
- Suspected security incidents must be reported within **24 hours** to the
  Security team. Good-faith reporting is never penalized, even when the
  reporter caused the incident.

## 5. Onboarding

Onboarding at Aurora follows five steps over your first 90 days:

1. **Day 1 — Orientation.** IT hands over your equipment; People Operations
   walks through payroll, this handbook, and your benefits enrollment window.
2. **Week 1 — Security training and access.** Complete mandatory security
   training and receive your hardware key (if applicable) before production
   access is granted.
3. **First 30 days — Buddy program.** Every new hire is assigned an
   **onboarding buddy for the first 30 days** — a peer outside your direct
   reporting line who answers the questions you don't want to ask your manager.
4. **30/60/90-day check-ins.** Structured check-ins with your manager at 30,
   60, and 90 days, with written goals set at the 30-day mark.
5. **Day 90 — Onboarding survey.** Feedback goes directly to People Operations
   and is reviewed by the executive team monthly.

Benefits enrollment closes **31 days after your start date**; missing the
window means waiting for the annual open enrollment in November.

## 6. Benefits

Retirement:

- Aurora offers a 401(k) (US) / RRSP match (Canada). Aurora matches **100% of
  the first 4%** of salary you contribute, plus **50% of the next 2%** — a
  maximum company match of 5% of salary.
- Matching contributions **vest immediately**. There is no cliff.

Health coverage:

- Aurora pays **85% of medical premiums** for employees and dependents on both
  available plans: the **Meridian Health PPO** and the **Meridian Health HMO**.
- **Dental and vision premiums are covered at 100%** for employees (dependents
  at 60%).
- A Health Savings Account (HSA) is available with the PPO high-deductible
  option; Aurora seeds it with **$750 per year**.

Other benefits:

- **Wellness stipend: $50 per month**, usable for gym memberships, therapy
  apps, or fitness equipment.
- **Learning and development budget: $1,500 per year** per employee for
  courses, books, and conferences, approved by your manager.
- Life insurance at 2x annual salary, employer-paid.
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) with 8 free counseling sessions per year.
- **Referral bonus: $3,000** for referred engineering hires and **$1,500** for
  all other roles, paid after the new hire completes 90 days.
- Commuter benefit of **$120 per month** in pre-tax transit or parking for
  office-based employees.

## 7. Compensation and Performance Reviews

Aurora targets the **65th percentile of market compensation** for every role,
benchmarked twice a year against the Radford and Pave surveys for our size and
sector. Salary bands are published internally for every level; your band, your
position in it, and the criteria for the next level are visible in Workline.

Performance reviews run on a **semi-annual cycle, closing in April and
October**. Each cycle combines a self-review, manager review, and two to four
peer reviews. Promotion decisions are made by a calibration committee, not by
individual managers, to keep standards consistent across departments.
Promotions are effective on the first payroll after the cycle closes and
include an adjustment to at least the midpoint of the new band.

Equity: all full-time employees receive stock options with a **4-year vesting
schedule and a 1-year cliff**, followed by monthly vesting. Refresh grants are
considered at each April cycle for employees rated "exceeds expectations" or
above. Departing employees have a **10-year post-termination exercise
window** — an unusually long window we adopted in 2023 so that leaving Aurora
never forces a rushed financial decision.

Annual bonuses apply to levels L6 and above and to all Sales roles. The
corporate bonus pool funds at 0–150% of target based on company revenue and
retention goals set each January by the board.

## 8. Equipment and IT Support

Every employee chooses between a **MacBook Pro 14-inch or a Lenovo ThinkPad X1**
at hire; laptops are refreshed on a **36-month cycle** or earlier if repair
costs exceed half the replacement price. Standard issue includes one external
monitor (up to 27 inches), keyboard, mouse, and headset. Engineers may request
a second monitor without approval; anything beyond the standard kit goes
through the IT equipment portal with manager sign-off.

IT support hours are **06:00–18:00 Pacific, Monday through Friday**, with an
on-call rotation for company-wide outages. The target first-response time for
standard tickets is **4 business hours**. Hardware failures are handled by
overnight replacement shipment for remote employees; office-based employees
can swap equipment same-day at the IT desk.

Software requests: anything on the pre-approved list in the portal installs
automatically. New vendor software requires a security review, which takes
**5 to 10 business days** — plan ahead. Browser extensions are allowlisted;
unlisted extensions are blocked by MDM policy.

## 9. Communication Norms

Aurora is a documentation-first company. Decisions of record live in the wiki,
not in chat. The norms below keep a hybrid company legible:

- **Slack response expectation: within 4 working hours** for direct mentions;
  urgent pages go through the on-call system, never through Slack DMs.
- Email is for external communication and formal notices only.
- Meetings require an agenda in the invite; meetings without one may be
  declined without explanation. **No-meeting Wednesdays** are company policy —
  recurring internal meetings may not be scheduled on Wednesdays.
- Every project keeps a single source-of-truth page with an accountable owner,
  updated at least every two weeks while the project is active.
- Recorded all-hands meetings are held **monthly on the first Thursday**, with
  anonymous Q&A; recordings and notes are posted within 24 hours for
  colleagues in other time zones.

Core collaboration hours company-wide are **10:00–14:00 Pacific**. Teams
outside the Americas define their own overlap window and publish it on their
team page.

## 10. Conduct and Miscellaneous

Aurora is an at-will employer in the US. We maintain a zero-tolerance policy
for harassment and discrimination; reports may be made to any manager, People
Operations, or the anonymous ethics line, and retaliation is itself a
terminable offense. Conflicts of interest — including outside employment with a
competitor and investments over 1% in a vendor — must be disclosed to Legal.

This handbook is reviewed every January. The current version always lives in
the People Operations wiki space; printed copies are uncontrolled. Nothing in
this handbook creates a contract of employment.
